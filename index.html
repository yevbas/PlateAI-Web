About PlateAI

An apple and a pie chart, because that's the whole idea.

The icon is the mission statement: real food, read as real data. PlateAI exists to close the gap between eating something and knowing what you just ate — without turning every meal into data entry.

The problem

Food tracking has always cost more than it gives back

Searching a database, weighing ingredients, scanning barcodes — every extra step is a reason to quit by day four. Most nutrition apps optimize for completeness. We optimized for the thing people actually do: take a photo before they eat.

The approach

Let the camera replace the spreadsheet

PlateAI reads a plate the way a nutritionist would glance at one — estimating portions and ingredients from the image itself, then staging the analysis so a usable number shows up before the detailed one finishes computing.

The team

Small, independent, and still shipping weekly

PlateAI is built by a small independent team who eats its own dog food — every macro ring, weight chart, and reminder shipped because someone on the team wanted it for themselves first.

What we optimize for

Four things we won't compromise on.

Speed

Seconds, not minutes

If logging a meal takes longer than eating it, the feature has already failed.

Accuracy

Numbers you can trust

Estimates are labeled as estimates, and refine automatically as richer analysis lands.

Privacy

Your plate is yours

Photos and logs exist to serve you, not to be resold as a training set for someone else.

Simplicity

One clear number

Every screen answers one question well instead of every question badly.

Support

How can we help?

Answers to what we hear most. Can't find it here — email us directly.

How accurate is the photo analysis?
Core macros arrive within seconds and are labeled as estimates based on what's visible in the photo. A second, batched pass refines micronutrients shortly after, so accuracy improves the longer you leave the entry open — but you're never blocked waiting for it.
Can I correct a meal if the AI gets it wrong?
Yes — every logged plate is editable. Adjust portion size, swap an ingredient, or enter macros manually, and PlateAI uses your correction for that entry going forward.
What does Weight Control do?
It's an optional module for logging weigh-ins and charting the trend over time, with weekly or monthly reminder notifications. Turn it on or off anytime from Settings — your tab bar updates instantly.
Is PlateAI free?
Yes, with a free tier covering core logging. A premium subscription unlocks higher daily limits and deeper insights — you'll see exactly what's included before you're asked to pay.
What happens to my food photos?
Photos are used to generate your nutrition estimate and are stored to power your history and statistics. They are not sold or used to train models for anyone outside of improving PlateAI itself.
I have a feature request — where does it go?
Straight to the team's inbox. We read every request and a good number of shipped features started as one, including the weekly weigh-in reminder.
